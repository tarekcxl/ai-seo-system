# AEO / GEO Optimization

A skill for optimizing content for modern AI-powered search experiences.

This skill helps improve visibility in:

- Google AI Overviews
- Google AI Mode
- ChatGPT
- Gemini
- Perplexity
- generative search systems
- answer engines
- retrieval-augmented generation (RAG) systems

The goal is to create content that is easy for both humans and AI systems to understand, extract, summarize, and cite.

---

## What this skill does

Use this skill to:

- optimize content for AI-generated search experiences
- improve citation likelihood in AI answers
- improve machine readability
- structure content for retrieval and summarization
- strengthen EEAT and authority signals
- improve answer extraction quality
- optimize content for conversational search behavior
- improve content clarity and factual grounding

## Important principle

AEO and GEO are not separate from SEO.

Modern AI search systems still rely heavily on:

- crawlability
- indexing
- technical SEO
- relevance
- authority
- content quality
- user usefulness

Good SEO remains the foundation of visibility in AI search experiences.

Do not attempt to manipulate AI systems with spammy tactics or artificial mentions.

## Core principles

### 1. Create non-commodity content

AI systems prefer content that provides:

- unique insights
- first-hand experience
- original analysis
- proprietary frameworks
- real examples
- expert perspectives

Avoid generic SEO filler content.

Commodity content is easier for AI systems to ignore or summarize away.

---

### 2. Optimize for retrieval and extraction

AI systems often retrieve and summarize small portions of pages.

Content should therefore be:

- well-structured
- easy to scan
- explicit
- logically organized
- context-rich

Good structure improves retrieval quality.

---

### 3. Prioritize answer clarity

AI systems favor content that directly answers questions.

Strong content:

- answers quickly
- avoids unnecessary fluff
- defines concepts clearly
- uses concise explanations
- provides contextual depth after the direct answer

---

### 4. Strengthen EEAT signals

Content should demonstrate:

- expertise
- experience
- authority
- trustworthiness

Add:

- examples
- references
- expert insights
- practical experience
- accurate claims
- supporting evidence

---

### 5. Optimize for conversational search

Users increasingly search using:

- questions
- long-tail conversational phrasing
- problem-focused prompts

Content should naturally address:

- “how”
- “why”
- “what”
- “best”
- “vs”
- “when”

queries.

---

## How AI search systems work

Modern AI search systems commonly use:

- retrieval-augmented generation (RAG)
- semantic retrieval
- query fan-out
- summarization
- answer synthesis

This means systems often:

- retrieve multiple sources
- compare information
- extract specific passages
- synthesize final responses

Content that is:

- structured
- factual
- explicit
- well-organized

is easier to retrieve and summarize.
