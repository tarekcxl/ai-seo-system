# CTR Optimization

A skill for improving click-through rate by optimizing titles and meta descriptions.

This skill focuses on making search results more compelling, differentiated, and aligned with search intent to increase clicks.

---

## What this skill does

Use this skill to:

- improve titles for higher CTR
- rewrite meta descriptions
- increase SERP attractiveness
- differentiate content from competitors
- identify weak SERP positioning
- improve search snippet relevance

---

## When to use this skill

Use this skill when:

- impressions are high but CTR is low
- titles feel weak or generic
- content is not getting enough clicks
- the user wants stronger headlines
- search snippets lack differentiation
- pages rank but underperform in clicks

---

## Core principles

- relevance beats cleverness
- clarity beats creativity
- strong value proposition increases clicks
- alignment with intent is critical
- differentiation improves visibility
- titles should compete directly against current SERP results

---

## SERP-first optimization (IMPORTANT)

Before rewriting titles or meta descriptions:

1. Search Google for the target keyword or query
2. Review the titles and meta descriptions of the top-ranking pages
3. Identify:
   - common wording patterns
   - repeated angles
   - overused phrases
   - missing opportunities
   - weak or generic messaging
4. Use SERP insights to create stronger and more differentiated recommendations

The goal is not to copy competitors.

The goal is to:
- understand what already exists
- identify patterns
- create titles that stand out while remaining relevant

---

## What to analyze in SERPs

Review:

- title structure
- use of numbers or dates
- wording patterns
- emotional triggers
- clarity
- specificity
- search intent alignment
- repetitive phrasing
- missing angles
- weak differentiation

Identify opportunities where:
- competitors sound generic
- titles are unclear
- intent is poorly matched
- stronger value propositions could improve CTR

---

## How to think about titles

Good titles:

- clearly match the query
- promise a benefit
- stand out from competitors
- avoid vague language
- feel immediately relevant
- create curiosity without clickbait

---

## Title improvement techniques

### 1. Clarity
Make the value obvious.

---

### 2. Specificity
Avoid generic phrasing.

Use:
- numbers
- outcomes
- concrete language
- clear promises

when appropriate.

---

### 3. Differentiation
Stand out from similar results.

Avoid producing titles that:
- sound identical to competitors
- repeat overused patterns
- feel templated

---

### 4. Intent match
Align exactly with what the searcher expects.

Match:
- informational intent
- commercial intent
- navigational intent
- comparison intent

---

### 5. SERP positioning
Think competitively.

Ask:
- why would someone click this result instead?
- what makes this title more useful or interesting?
- what angle is missing from the SERP?

---

## Meta description guidelines

Meta descriptions should:

- reinforce the title
- expand on the value proposition
- encourage clicks
- remain clear and direct
- support search intent
- differentiate from competing snippets

Avoid:
- keyword stuffing
- generic summaries
- vague marketing language

---

## Optimization workflow

### Step 1: Understand the query
Identify:
- target keyword
- search intent
- target audience
- page goal

---

### Step 2: Analyze SERPs
Search Google for the keyword and review:
- titles
- meta descriptions
- recurring patterns
- missing opportunities

---

### Step 3: Identify weaknesses
Determine whether the current title/meta:
- lacks clarity
- lacks specificity
- lacks differentiation
- mismatches intent
- feels generic
- undercommunicates value

---

### Step 4: Create improved variations
Generate:
- multiple title options
- multiple meta description options
- different positioning angles when useful

---

### Step 5: Prioritize strongest options
Recommend the best-performing candidates based on:
- clarity
- differentiation
- intent match
- CTR potential
- SERP competitiveness

---

## Output format

Provide:

### Titles
- 3–5 improved title options

### Meta descriptions
- 2–3 improved options

### SERP insights
Briefly explain:
- common SERP patterns
- differentiation opportunities
- why the recommended options are stronger

---

## Output quality bar

Strong output:
- clear and compelling
- aligned with intent
- differentiated from competitors
- informed by SERP analysis
- easy to understand
- likely to increase clicks

Weak output:
- generic
- vague
- repetitive
- too similar to competitors
- not informed by SERP context

## Boundaries

This skill focuses on CTR optimization only.

It does not:
- rewrite full content
- perform deep SEO analysis
- define full content strategy

Use:
- `content-optimization`
- `seo-content-strategy`
- `seo-data-analysis`

when broader SEO work is required.

---

## Working style

Be sharp, strategic, and competitive.

Think like someone optimizing against the actual SERP — not writing titles in isolation.

Optimize for clicks without misleading the user.
