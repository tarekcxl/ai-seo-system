# Content Optimization

A skill for improving existing content based on performance data, SEO insights, and content briefs.

The goal is to increase performance by improving:
- CTR (how often users click)
- rankings (how well the page competes)
- clarity and usefulness (how well the content satisfies intent)

---

## What this skill does

Use this skill to:

- improve titles and meta descriptions for higher CTR
- rewrite weak or unclear sections
- update blog content based on briefs or insights
- align content with search intent
- improve structure, readability, and depth
- apply competitor insights to strengthen positioning

---

## When to use this skill

Use this skill when the user wants to:

- improve CTR for high-impression pages
- rewrite or enhance blog content
- update an article based on SEO insights or a brief
- improve rankings through content updates
- optimize titles, intros, or structure
- make content clearer, more engaging, or more useful

Trigger even if the user says things like:
- “this page isn’t performing”
- “CTR is low”
- “update this blog”
- “rewrite this section”

---

## Core principles

- Optimize based on context (data, intent, or brief)
- Do not rewrite blindly — improve strategically
- Preserve what already works
- Focus on clarity, usefulness, and intent alignment
- Strong structure beats unnecessary length

---

## Inputs

This skill may receive:

- SEO insights (CTR, rankings, impressions)
- competitor insights
- content briefs
- existing blog content

If multiple inputs exist, prioritize:
1. search intent
2. performance signals
3. competitor gaps

---

## Optimization types

### 1. CTR optimization
Use when:
- impressions are high
- CTR is low

Focus on:
- stronger value proposition
- clearer intent match
- more compelling framing

Output:
- 3–5 title options
- 2–3 meta description options

---

### 2. Content rewriting
Use when:
- sections are unclear or weak
- explanations lack depth

Focus on:
- clarity
- flow
- better explanations

---

### 3. Content updates (from briefs)
Use when:
- a structured brief exists
- gaps or improvements are identified

Focus on:
- adding missing sections
- improving depth
- restructuring content

---

### 4. Intent alignment
Use when:
- content does not match what users expect

Focus on:
- format (guide, list, comparison)
- direct answers early in the content
- stronger intro and structure

---

## How to work

### Step 1: Understand context
Identify:
- goal (CTR, ranking, clarity)
- available inputs (data, brief, content)

---

### Step 2: Identify weaknesses
Look for:
- weak titles or intros
- unclear structure
- missing depth
- intent mismatch

---

### Step 3: Apply improvements
- focus on high-impact changes
- avoid unnecessary rewriting
- preserve strong sections

---

### Step 4: Deliver output
Provide optimized content with clear improvements.

## Output quality bar

Strong output:
- improves performance drivers (CTR, ranking, clarity)
- aligns with intent and structure
- is clear, structured, and useful

Weak output:
- rewrites everything without reason
- adds fluff instead of clarity
- ignores data or intent

---

## Boundaries

This skill improves existing content.

It does not:
- perform SEO data analysis
- decide overall content strategy
- do deep competitor breakdowns

---

## Working style

Be practical and focused.

Prioritize the changes most likely to improve performance.
