# SEO Content Strategy

A skill for deciding what content to create or update based on search demand, intent, and competitive context.

This skill transforms SEO opportunities into clear content decisions and structured briefs.

---

## What this skill does

Use this skill to:

- identify new content opportunities
- perform keyword and topic research
- understand search intent
- decide whether to create or update content
- define content angles and positioning
- create structured content briefs

---

## When to use this skill

Use this skill when the user wants to:

- find topics to write about
- perform keyword research
- decide what content to create
- turn SEO insights into content plans
- create structured blog outlines
- define how to approach a topic

Trigger when the user says things like:
- “what should we write about”
- “find content opportunities”
- “keyword research”
- “create a content brief”
- “how do we rank for this topic”

---

## Core principles

- Focus on intent, not just keywords
- Demand must exist (validated by data)
- One page = one primary intent
- Differentiation is required to win
- Content should solve the query better than competitors

---

## Inputs

This skill may receive:

- SEO opportunities (from seo-analyst)
- competitor insights
- keywords or topics
- business goals or priorities

---

## How to think about keyword research

Do not treat keywords as a list.

Instead:

### Step 1: Identify core topic
Group keywords into a single topic.

Avoid:
- creating multiple pages for the same intent

---

### Step 2: Understand search intent

Classify intent as:

- informational (learn)
- commercial (compare, evaluate)
- transactional (buy, act)

Your content must match the dominant intent.

---

### Step 3: Evaluate opportunity

Look for:

- meaningful search demand
- realistic ranking potential
- relevance to business goals

---

### Step 4: Analyze competitors

Use competitor insights to:

- understand content expectations
- identify gaps
- find differentiation opportunities

---

## Decision framework

### Create new content when:
- intent is not fully covered by existing pages
- the topic deserves its own page
- demand is strong

---

### Update existing content when:
- a page already ranks for the topic
- improvements can push rankings higher
- intent overlap exists

---

## Content brief structure

When creating a brief, ALWAYS include:

### Title
- aligned with search intent
- clear and specific

### Target query
- primary keyword/topic

### Content type
- new or update

### Search intent
- informational / commercial / transactional

### Content angle
- how this content will stand out

### Key sections
- main headings (H2s)

### Differentiation
- what will make this better than competitors

---

## Output guidelines

Provide structured outputs such as:

- content ideas (with reasoning)
- prioritized topic lists
- full content briefs

---

## Output quality bar

Strong output:
- based on real demand and intent
- clearly differentiated
- structured and actionable

Weak output:
- generic topic suggestions
- keyword lists without context
- no clear angle or structure

---

## Boundaries

This skill defines WHAT to create and HOW to approach it.

It does not:
- perform deep content rewriting
- execute content updates
- analyze raw SEO datasets

---

## Working style

Be strategic and focused.

Avoid generic recommendations. Every suggestion should have a clear reason.
