## Tone by Content Type

### Blog Posts & Long-form Articles
- Lead with a provocative claim or counterintuitive insight.
- Use subheadings as standalone statements (not just labels).
- Write conclusions that push readers toward the next action.
- Appropriate use of "I" in opinion pieces; use "we" for institutional perspective.

### Email
- Subject lines: benefit-forward, specific numbers preferred.
  - ✅ "Why 80% of email tests reach the wrong conclusion"
  - ❌ "Our latest blog post is live"
- Preview text should extend, not repeat, the subject line.
- One idea per email. One CTA per email.

### Social Media (LinkedIn)
- First line must stop the scroll: make it a bold statement or surprising stat.
- Use line breaks liberally for readability.
- End with a question or explicit CTA to drive engagement.
- Avoid hashtag spam. Maximum 3 relevant hashtags.

### Paid Advertising
- Headline: problem or aspiration in under 8 words.
- Body: one proof point or specific benefit.
- CTA: action verb + specific outcome (e.g., "Get the free framework" not "Learn more").

## Words and Phrases to Use

<!-- REPLACE: Add terminology and phrases that are distinctly "CXL." -->

- Practitioners / practitioners-first
- Evidence-based / data-driven (only when we actually have data)
- Actionable
- The research shows…
- Here's what actually works…
- Let's be direct…

## Words and Phrases to Avoid

<!-- REPLACE: Add terms your brand has banned or that feel off-voice. -->

- Guru / ninja / rockstar (when referring to people)
- Disruptive / game-changing / revolutionary
- Leverage (as a verb)
- Synergy / synergistic
- Best-in-class (without evidence)
- Seamless / frictionless (without specifics)

## Punctuation Rule: No Em Dashes

**Do not use em dashes (—).** They are a hallmark of AI-generated content. Restructure the sentence using a comma, full stop, or colon instead.
