![Unique selling proposition examples](https://cxl.com/wp-content/uploads/2022/06/Unique-selling-proposition-examples-1280x670.png)

Your [unique selling proposition](https://cxl.com/blog/unique-selling-proposition/) (USP) separates you from the competition. It’s a statement that communicates your strengths and benefits over your competition’s. A good one makes your product or brand stand out amongst the noise.

Similar to a [unique value proposition](https://cxl.com/blog/value-proposition-examples-how-to-create/) (UVP), for customers, it’s a reason to trust and choose you ahead of someone else. For you, it’s the linchpin that powers your sales and marketing efforts.

USP and UVP are occasionally used interchangeably; but they shouldn’t be. The two are similar, but have different purposes. A unique selling proposition focuses on what separates you from the competition. In comparison, a unique value proposition focuses solely on the benefit that you provide to the customer.

But what does a USP look like?

In this article, we’ll demonstrate what makes an effective USP by sharing some of the best examples from the worlds of [B2B marketing](https://cxl.com/institute/programs/b2b-marketing-and-growth/#h-learn-b2b-marketing-that-works-for-how-people-buy-today), e-commerce, and DTC.

You’ll learn why and how these brands get it right so you can follow their lead to unearth your own competitive advantage.

## Examples of effective unique selling propositions in B2B [Anchor link for: h examples of effective unique selling propositions in b2b](https://cxl.com/blog/unique-selling-proposition-examples/\#h-examples-of-effective-unique-selling-propositions-in-b2b)

1. Stripe

![Screenshot of Stripe Homepage](https://lh7-rt.googleusercontent.com/docsz/AD_4nXdeBJoZa_lg4-0hlQN7XKG3y_tos837b6QxB-W5V1hNUuxTnvuF3OcMN-ShQYlo0GfeOgFtbEdb9oUIVpiGVIEemSkPbFuMhpIhQk55agTpAnqeJIk-1nWLPj84At5Qhgo3CTtW?key=zVqJ5LiXE2TS0MvpXPxNPZqY)

[Stripe](https://stripe.com/gb) is an online payment processing provider that allows businesses to accept payments from their customers.

It operates in the same market as PayPal, yet distinctly stands out. How? By going after developers.

Rather than simply providing a payment gateway like PayPal that highlights ease of transactions, Stripe offers _“Financial infrastructure to grow your revenue.”_ It presents itself as a **financial infrastructure** provider aimed at developers, enterprises, and fast-growing startups looking for seamless integrations and advanced capabilities.

It doesn’t just help businesses accept payments; it provides a powerful infrastructure that fuels **revenue growth**. This positions Stripe as a **long-term partner** for businesses looking to scale, rather than just a transactional tool like many competitors.

_Join the millions of companies that use Stripe to accept payments online and in person, embed financial services, power custom revenue models, and build a more profitable business._

[_Stripe website_](https://stripe.com/)

This level of control isn’t for everyone. But for developers and business owners who want to get hands-on with payments processes, Stripe’s software and APIs offer full control, eliminating the hassle of bringing together disparate systems.

Stripe is a company that knows its target audience—and its USP reflects this. This helps them filter out people who aren’t suitable as much as it attracts those who are.

2. Fullstory

![Screenshot of Fullstory Homepage](https://lh7-rt.googleusercontent.com/docsz/AD_4nXfwP5ni31oZ2jhIKhgDjMvaLkBHDt2_kj3JqflF8w9MV4wtVzbCYw050sT3PwBjQpipDp_MLZqf4iaAhqEiErfttr6ftA9UVQQ-9cDVZTJ7Xbzbcp5D3b2bGCTzqb0a2jVtthnw?key=zVqJ5LiXE2TS0MvpXPxNPZqY)

[**Fullstory**](https://www.fullstory.com/) is a digital experience analytics platform that helps businesses understand how users interact with their websites and apps. It’s USP, _“From click to clarity,”_ communicates a clear transformation: taking raw user interactions (“click”) and turning them into actionable insights (“clarity”).

The simplicity makes it memorable and easy to understand in an industry full of jargon.

Fullstory’s USP is compelling because it offers **real-time insights and predictive analytics**, enabling businesses to make fast, data-driven decisions and proactively optimize user experiences.

By targeting **enterprise-level customers**, it assures scalability and reliability for large organizations handling complex data, while its focus on **driving results** highlights tangible outcomes such as increased conversions, improved engagement, and better customer retention.

This combination of speed, foresight, and measurable business impact makes Fullstory a powerful tool for enterprise growth.

3. Spotify for Creators

![Screenshot of Spotify for Creators Homepage](<Base64-Image-Removed>)

What used to be Anchor, now Spotify’s podcasting tool, [Spotify for Creators](https://podcasters.spotify.com/) gets straight to the point:

**_Make your show the next big thing_**

_Powerful tools from Spotify to manage and grow your audio or video podcast.._

_Spotify for Creators_

It speaks directly to the aspirations of podcasters, focusing on their desire for growth and success. With powerful tools for both audio and video podcasts, Spotify positions itself as more than just a hosting platform—it’s a comprehensive solution that helps creators manage and grow their content.

By emphasizing growth and offering tailored, effective resources, Spotify becomes a crucial partner for podcasters looking to elevate their shows.

4. Canva Teams

![Screenshot of Canva Teams Homepage](https://lh7-rt.googleusercontent.com/docsz/AD_4nXc7Q1D9IrEk_bDZAldUHzu1KQwEca2Ii0jYCmvSzmh0e5voiXisbOR3Zg9Y1KkdLYhNOfuFsrXiAVpqeLfe1JwfmHb7KNzVDQpdk7RdxllRmYjqqlEMOy58qW03Sp-cMXbobU8sew?key=zVqJ5LiXE2TS0MvpXPxNPZqY)

[Canva](https://www.canva.com/about/) is an online design and publishing platform aimed at making it easy for anyone to create and share vector graphics. Its business USP highlights collaboration as a core value, providing tools that enable seamless teamwork, creativity, and high-quality output. :

_“Canva for your business”_

_“Where teams create their best work, together.”_

It’s a nod to the simplicity of Canva’s tools. It also separates the platform from competitors like Adobe PhotoShop and Illustrator, and ProCreate, whose products are aimed towards experienced artists.

While rival tools offer more advanced editing features, they come with a steeper learning curve. Canva’s drag-and-drop shapes and elements let complete novices design a professional image, flyer, or document in minutes.

By understanding its place in the market, they’ve been able to unearth a competitive advantage. It’s a lesson in how to turn what could be seen as a weakness into a strength.

5. Kit (Formerly ConvertKit)

![Screenshot of Kit Homepage](https://lh7-rt.googleusercontent.com/docsz/AD_4nXe64ryxzuMvYMTweXanDfnCaEjpniwzuH_JFCPf8mO_nwlG53koeVIz0tJVWio9QdPuyUkJWmHU8HnJaQLXUP55Mr6cttW_-Y5cDfon1aZUOX3W_jcfIQlOxxuD8DlUlRfRd8Ms?key=zVqJ5LiXE2TS0MvpXPxNPZqY)

On the surface, there’s not much to separate [Kit](https://convertkit.com/) from other email marketing platforms like MailChimp or iContact. Yet, they’re a SaaS brand that generates over $2 million in monthly recurring revenue every month.

How? Because they know their target audience. Kit provides email marketing tools for independent creators, a segment of the target market only they honed in on when they launched.

This audience is a central feature of their USP:

_“For creators who mean business. “_

_“Kit is the email-first operating system for creators building a valuable business”_

_Kit website_

**What does Kit offer that’s different?** It’s focused on **serious** creators—those looking to build sustainable businesses, rather than just casual hobbyists. It speaks directly to their goals of monetizing and growing a business, not just creating content.

Just a quick scroll takes you to another USP that sets it apart:

“Build a business with purpose.”

**How is it different from its competitors?** It’s made by other creators for creators with purpose. The implication is that Kit understands and supports creators better than other platforms because of this.

6. GoTo Webinar

![Screenshot of GoTo Webinar Homepage](<Base64-Image-Removed>)

GoTo has pinpointed the key problems that people often have with webinar software: difficult to use and full of minor issues. So its USP is simple:

_Hassle-free webinar software… makes events easy._

_GoTo website_

To boost the impression, it makes sure visitors know they can try it before they buy. No drama; just ease of use.

7. SoundCloud

![Screenshot of SoundCloud Homepage](<Base64-Image-Removed>)

[Soundcloud](https://soundcloud.com/) has become renowned as a place for creators and fans to connect, share music, and discover new tracks, raw demos, and podcasts. The community aspect of the platform helps it stand out from the likes of Spotify and Apple Music, where artists and fans are separate. This is communicated in its USP:

_“Discover. Get Discovered.”_

_“Discover your next obsession, or become someone else’s._

_SoundCloud is the only community where fans and artists come together to discover and connect through music.”_

But it doesn’t stop there. Soundcloud has a front-page carousel, with another USP aimed specifically at creators:

![](<Base64-Image-Removed>)

“It all starts with an upload.”

“From bedrooms and broom closets to studios and stadiums, SoundCloud is where you define what’s next in music. Just hit upload.”

Not only do they tailor their value proposition at the prospect, they [tell a story](https://cxl.com/blog/storytelling/). SoundCloud knows that artists who make it big will likely monetize their music with a record label or streaming platform. It capitalizes on being a place to grow a word-of-mouth following.

Like Canva, it’s an example of knowing where you fit in the market and using it to your advantage.

## Examples of unique selling propositions in e-commerce [Anchor link for: h examples of unique selling propositions in e commerce](https://cxl.com/blog/unique-selling-proposition-examples/\#h-examples-of-unique-selling-propositions-in-e-commerce)

8. Death Wish Coffee

![Screenshot of Death Wish Coffee Homepage](<Base64-Image-Removed>)

In a market dominated by rich blends and smooth tastes, [Death Wish Coffee](https://www.deathwishcoffee.com/) bucks the trend:

_“If You’re Not First You’re Last.”_

_“Death Wish Coffee is the #1 Organic and #1 Fair Trade Coffee brand in the US. Next stop? Total world domination.”_

Its USP instantly tells you that this coffee packs a punch. Those looking for a smooth taste are probably better served elsewhere.

Claiming to be the ‘#1’  or “greatest” anything is a risky strategy—one that means being able to walk the walk as well as you talk the talk. Death Wish Coffee backs itself by showing how their coffee is made.

What’s more, the company’s branding, marketing campaigns, tone of voice, and services all align with its USP.  The bolder the claim, the more likely it is to be challenged. Make sure you can back up your words. Your brand should live and breathe its USP.

9. Patagonia

![Screenshot of Patagonia Homepage](<Base64-Image-Removed>)

[Patagonia’s](https://www.patagonia.com.au/pages/our-mission) USP isn’t a product or a feature, it’s their reason for existing:

_“Self-Guided”_

_“Innovation Built on Exploration”_

This USP sets Patagonia apart from the competition by establishing itself as more than a clothing brand. It’s a lifestyle and movement dedicated to making a difference. In one sentence, Patagonia sums up a number of its unique selling points:

- **“Self-Guided”** speaks to the spirit of independence and personal exploration, core values of Patagonia’s target audience.
- **“Innovation Built on Exploration”** emphasizes how Patagonia uses their deep knowledge of the outdoors and exploration to drive their product development.

![Screenshot of Patagonia's USP on Homepage](<Base64-Image-Removed>)

Patagonia USP also includes activism, sustainability, and responsibitly (for its products but their impact on the planet).

It’s a USP that appeals to someone, rather than everyone, which is what all great USPs should do.

10.  Saddleback Leather

![Screenshot of Saddleback Leather's Homepage](<Base64-Image-Removed>)

_“They’ll fight over it when you’re dead”_

[Saddleback Leather’s](https://saddlebackleather.com/) tagline conveys the unique value of its products in a single line. These are products that will outlive you.

It’s backed up by substance (each Saddleback leather product is over-engineered, comes with no breakable parts, and is backed by a 100-year warranty) and great storytelling.

![Screenshot of Saddleback Leather's USP](<Base64-Image-Removed>)

When knock-off Saddleback Leather briefcases started appearing on the market, the company also created a ‘ [how it’s made’ video](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=a11wlngpuSY) to show off the product quality and add weight to its USP.

Like your selling points, your story is unique to you. By using storytelling to craft your USPs, you’ll stand a better chance of creating a memorable message.

11.  TOMS

![Screenshot of TOMS' Homepage](<Base64-Image-Removed>)

[TOMS](https://www.toms.com/) Shoes was one of the first shoe companies in the world to use a buy one, gift one model. For every pair of TOMS sold, another pair was donated to a child in need.

For a long-time, this was the company’s USP. As other companies have started to offer a similar model, TOMS’ USP has evolved. Today, the company commits one-third of its profits for grassroots goods.

![Screenshot of TOMS' USP](<Base64-Image-Removed>)

But what made the one-for-one model so successful as a USP remains.

_“A Fresh Start”_

It taps into our want to give ourselves a fresh start but also help others, and makes it easy to do so. Customers can buy shoes and feel good about doing it.

Building a USP around a cause may run the risk of alienating some people. But in uniting the company, customer, and purpose, you can create brand loyalty that lasts a lifetime.

12.  Bee’s Wrap

![Screenshot of bee's Wrap Homepage](<Base64-Image-Removed>)

[Bee’s Wrap](https://www.beeswrap.com/) is an e-commerce business that produces sustainable food wrap made from organic cotton and beeswax. It’s an outlier in a market of foil and plastic wrap. But even uniqueness needs a USP.

Bee’s Wrap taps into an important global topic: plastic waste.

_“Goodbye Plastic, Hello Magic”_

The USP promotes Bee’s Wrap as an alternative to single-use plastics and a long-term solution to the problem of plastic pollution.

But this surface-level wordplay does more than appeal to eco-conscious customers. It goes beyond the product, reflecting the company’s standing as a B Corp and Green America certified company committed to social and environmental change.

Whether your USP is [product, purpose, or prospect-led](https://cxl.com/blog/types-value-propositions/), look at the big picture. Make sure your messaging represents every aspect of your brand.

13. Huel

![Screenshot of Huel's Homepage](<Base64-Image-Removed>)

Huel is a brand offering nutritionally complete meal replacement products designed for health-conscious individuals seeking a convenient, balanced, and nutritious meal replacement solution for their daily needs.

_“Fast, nutritious, complete food. Designed by expert nutritionists as a convenient way to get all the daily nutrients you need”_

Its USP is clear and concise, appealing to customers’ needs—a well-rounded meal option formulated by nutrition experts that’s quick, cost effective, and convenient. The USP speaks to people who are busy but care about their health (like professionals, fitness enthusiasts, or people with hectic lifestyles).

The brand also appeals to those looking for plant-based products with low carbon footprints.

![Screenshot of Huel's USP on Homepage](<Base64-Image-Removed>)

14.  ASKET

![Screenshot of Asket Homepage](<Base64-Image-Removed>)

It’s unusual to hear a fashion brand talking about how the world doesn’t need another fashion brand, but [ASKET’s](https://www.asket.com/) USP taps into the zeitgeist of conscious consumption.

ASKET wants people to buy less and it promotes this in its USP by promising meaningful essentials that are traceable so customers know where their money goes.

_“The Pursuit of Less”_

_“The world doesn’t need another fashion brand. Garments, products of delicate labour and precious resources, have lost their value. We buy more and use them less than ever – packing our wardrobes, filling landfills and fueling incinerators._

_“Our promise is to restore the value of garments by creating meaningful essentials: A permanent collection of zero-compromise pieces, their stories uncovered and told. We can’t live without clothing, but we can make the pieces we choose to invest in count.”_

![Screenshot of Asket's USP on Homepage](<Base64-Image-Removed>)

For consumers who monitor their carbon footprint, ASKET is a company you can feel proud to support. That’s a powerful thing in building a lasting brand.

When crafting your USP, listen to the conversations taking place in your industry. Find what your potential customers are passionate about and use their insights to guide your specific benefit.

## Examples of unique selling propositions by Direct-to-Consumer (DTC) brands [Anchor link for: h examples of unique selling propositions by direct to consumer dtc brands](https://cxl.com/blog/unique-selling-proposition-examples/\#h-examples-of-unique-selling-propositions-by-direct-to-consumer-dtc-brands)

15.  Billie

![Screenshot of Billie's'Homepage](<Base64-Image-Removed>)

Reading the [Billie](https://mybillie.com/pages/about)‘s USP makes it immediately clear what the brand is about:

_“We’re out to build a better shelf”_

_“It started with a razor that changed everything: We noticed that women were overpaying for razors and shamed for having body hair. Kind of a double whammy, when you think about it. So, we did away with the Pink Tax and put body hair on the big screen._

_Now, meet our sparkling clean collection of better beauty basics for your medicine cabinet (and handbag and gym bag and desk drawer). Powered by ingredients that do no harm, work super hard, and take the guesswork out of clean.”_

Billie is an online store and subscription service built for women who are fed up with the current state of sanitary products. It highlights this in its USP by following the problem-solution formula:

**_Problem:_** _“We noticed that women were overpaying for razors”_

**_Solution:_** _“Meet our sparkling clean collection of better beauty basics”_

_“We’re out to build a better shelf” boils down that formula into a single line. Things aren’t right, so they’re doing something about it._

Run surveys, read reviews, and conduct market and [user research](https://cxl.com/blog/user-research-culture/) to find the problems customers are having. Then, base your USP around your solution.

16.  Warby Parker

![Screenshot of Warby Parker's Homepage](<Base64-Image-Removed>)

[Warby Parker](https://www.warbyparker.com/) is an outlier as an online retailer in that it went from [click-to-brick](https://multichannelmerchant.com/ecommerce/click-brick-success-story-warby-parker-continues-disrupt-innovate/). By expanding its e-commerce business to physical stores, they’ve been able to increase accessibility and meet customer needs online and offline.

Its USP is rooted in its ability to offer a seamless customer experience:

_“Try 5 frames at home for free”_

With a virtual try-on service, customers choose five frames to test at home for free.

Offering five frames rather than one means they’re able to rival the physical store experience, where you’d typically try on multiple frames. It also brings frames to you, eliminating the need to travel.

This USP disrupts the market, alleviating a pain point and putting customer service at the forefront of the offer. While a USP is about what you do differently from everyone else, always make it about the customer.

17.  Hiut Denim

![Screenshot from Hiut Jeans' website. Text says: Do One Thing Well. We make jeans. That’s it. Nothing else. No distractions. Nothing to steal our focus. No kidding ourselves that we can be good at everything. No trying to conquer the whole world. We will just do our best to conqueror bit of it. So each day we will come in and make the best jeans we know how. Use the best quality denim from artisan mills from all around the world. Cut them with an expert eye. And then let our Grand Masters behind the sewing machines do the rest. There is a great deal of satisfaction to be gained from making something well, of such superior quality that you know it is going to stand the test of time. It makes the hard work and the obsessing over each and every detail worth all the effort. That’s our reward. So no bobble hats, no sweatshirts, no jumpers. Just the best pair of jeans that time and skill will allow. The more classic you can make something, the longer it will last. – Paul Arden ](<Base64-Image-Removed>)

In a fashion market filled with brands that produce multiple items, [Hiut Denim](https://hiutdenim.co.uk/) is a small business bucking the trend.

_“We make jeans. That’s it. Nothing else. No distractions. We focus all our talents on jeans. And we are proud to say that we make some of the best jeans in the world.”_

This USP works as an offer, a mission statement, and a brand story. It’s honest and tells the customer Hiut Denim is a brand that obsesses over every detail of every pair of jeans. No half measures.

It’s backed up by the company’s in-house manufacturing process and a website that breaks down exactly where and how jeans are made.

When it comes to crafting a USP that stands the test of time, finding the one thing you do well is a good path to follow.

18.  Lemonade

![Screenshot of Lemonade on Homepage](<Base64-Image-Removed>)

[Lemonade’s](https://www.lemonade.com/) USP is an example of a brand that knows who it’s talking to. It leads with challenging the status quo:

_“Forget everything you know about insurance”“We can’t fix your lives but we can fix your activewear. Great fit. Premium fabrics. Tech you actually need. And stylish as hell. You’re welcome.”_

This immediately grabs attention and presents their Lemonade as an innovative modern alternative. By challenging traditional perceptions of insurance, the USP suggests they’re offering something radically different from the old, bureaucratic, often frustrating insurance model.

It appeals to clients who value customer services, affordability, speed, and trust. It also taps into the emotional aspect by donating any leftover cash to various causes, positioning itself as a company that cares about more than profits.

![Screenshot of Lemonade's USP](<Base64-Image-Removed>)

19.  HelloFresh

![Screenshot of Hello Fresh Homepage](<Base64-Image-Removed>)

[HelloFresh’s](https://www.hellofresh.com/) USP is a bold statement that establishes them as the best at what they do:

_“America’s #1 Meal Kit”_

This tells us that HelloFresh is trusted while delivering social proof: if it’s the most popular meal kit in America, it _has_ to be good. It also creates FOMO: you wouldn’t want to miss out on something this popular.

Right above that statement are reasons why HelloFresh is so popular. It’s quick and easy, saving time and effort —the main reasons why you’d look to invest in meal kits.

If you’re the best at what you do and you can communicate it, don’t be afraid to go bold with your USP.

20.  Patch

![Screenshot of Patch's Homepage](<Base64-Image-Removed>)

[Patch](https://www.patchplants.com/gb/en/) is a company that aims to bring the joy of gardening to everyone. They recommend, source, and deliver plants to customers’ homes and provide tips to help look after them.

It was born out of its founders’ struggle to a) find the right plants for his home and b) keep them alive.

The company’s USP communicates these struggles and how it solves them:

_“From expert green thumbs to new plant parents, sun traps to shady corners, we have a leafy friend for every setting.”_

_“Find the plants for you and we’ll help you welcome them home, with UK-wide delivery and simple care tips.”_

It shows that if your business model plugs a gap in the market, your USP can be as simple as explaining your offer.

Dig down into the roots of your company. Why do you exist? The reason might well be your USP.

21.  Taylor Stitch

![Screenshot of Taylor Stitch's Homepage](<Base64-Image-Removed>)

[Taylor Stitch](https://www.taylorstitch.com/) is a clothing brand that uses crowdfunding to design products. Crowdfunding in itself is not a unique selling point. Lots of brands successfully fund products in this way. But it’s _how_ [Taylor Stitch uses crowdfunding](https://www.makersights.com/case-study/taylor-stitch-case-study) that helps them stand out.

_“We design new products. You crowd fund them and save 20%. Our planet takes on less waste. We deliver them when they’re seasonally appropriate. Everybody wins.”_

This USP makes it clear why customers benefit from crowdfunding. You save money, help the environment, and get clothes when they’re needed. What’s not to like? It also highlights the social good of this business model, making it a brand you’d want to support.

Taylor Stitch is an example of how finding the right angle can turn a common approach into a unique marketing strategy.

## Conclusion [Anchor link for: h conclusion](https://cxl.com/blog/unique-selling-proposition-examples/\#h-conclusion)

Whether they’re based around product, service, prospect, or purpose, each of these [unique selling propositions](https://cxl.com/blog/unique-selling-proposition/) examples is the result of research and testing.

To find your unique angle, ask questions of your audience and drill down into your niche to uncover gaps in the market. Use research to guide your copy, then write and rewrite until you can confidently state your USP in a few sentences.

From there, [test your USP](https://wynter.com/) with your audience. Check that it resonates and, more importantly, makes a difference to your bottom line.
